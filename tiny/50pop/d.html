Installing OpenBSD 6.0 on Digital Ocean

Vultr is easier, but if you really prefer Digital Ocean, I got this great tip from Jason Tubnor:

  1. Sign up or log in at digitalocean.com then click [Create Droplet]
  2. Choose an image → Distributions → FreeBSD 10.3. Then any size and data center. No need for the additional options.
  3. Add your SSH keys. If you've already uploaded one, check its box. If not, upload one now.
  4. 1 Droplet. Any hostname. Then [Create]. Wait a minute while it creates.
  5. Copy the IP address of your new Droplet, then from your computer: ssh freebsd@___.__.__.__ (that IP address). That should log you in to a “Welcome to FreeBSD” screen and prompt.
  6. sudo su -
  7. fetch ftp://ftp3.usa.openbsd.org/pub/OpenBSD/6.0/amd64/miniroot60.fs (or closer mirror)
  8. swapoff /dev/gpt/swapfs
  9. sysctl kern.geom.debugflags=0x10
  10. dd if=miniroot60.fs of=/dev/vtbd0 bs=512k
  11. reboot
  12. Go back to https://cloud.digitalocean.com/droplets and to the right of your Droplet name, click the More ↓ menu, then Access Console.
  13. That should pop up a new window with a black screen OpenBSD installation program.

Then go to the installation guide.
